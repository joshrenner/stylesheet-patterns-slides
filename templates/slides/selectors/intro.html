CSS gets evaluated from right to left.

The evaluation of any more than a single element to determine styling is inefficient.

The four rules Google Page Speed recommendations consider inefficient:

  • Rules with descendant selectors.
    E.g. #content h3
  • Rules with child or adjacent selectors.
    E.g. #content > h3
  • Rules with overly qualified selectors.
    E.g. div#content > h3
  • Rules that apply :hover to non-link elements.
    E.g. div#content:hover
